                          Cracked Films Presents


                              BLACK EYED DOG

                                                         
Black Eyed Dog (originally called Apathy) was originally meant as a leaving 
present for some friends of mine, but I have taken the film further, 
because it came out much better than I had hoped for. It looks at a 
character (Guy) who suffers from phonophobia (fear of talking or being 
talked to), and the frustration and anger that this can cause. Of course, 
being a horror film, there is also a killer in it as well! The killer is 
born out of this anger and frustration, and when his friend gets annoyed at
him for being unable to socialise with him, this anger attacks and kills 
his friend. When he encounters this anger himself, he expects himself, and 
takes his own life.The film is never implicate about the exact mental 
disability (phonophobia), only showing the characters reaction, rather than
the cause of it. On this level, it is a very personal film to me.

Cracked Films is an international collective of digital filmmakers pooling 
their resources and talents to create a diverse body of work. Its goals 
are firmly placed in exploring and exploiting the new forms of production, 
distribution and exhibition made possible through advances in technology 
and mass communications.

We aim to avoid conventional forms of distribution and exhibition; instead 
we wish to find an audience based on word of mouth recommendations, free 
distribution and exhibition via DVD, underground screenings and especially
Bit-torrents.

With its headquarters based in the less trendy parts of East London, 
Cracked Films produces work that aims to be expressive and reflective of 
its authors and their experiences.
