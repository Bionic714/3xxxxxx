Robert Nesta Marley, OM (February 6, 1945 ? May 11, 1981), better known as Bob Marley, was a Jamaican singer, songwriter, and guitarist. He is the most widely known performer of reggae music, and is famous for having popularized the genre outside Jamaica. A faithful Rastafari, Marley is regarded by many as a prophet of the religion,[1] as well as one of the greatest songwriters of all time.[2]

His best known songs are a mixture of reggae, rock, and rhythm and blues, and include \"I Shot the Sheriff\", made famous in 1974 by Eric Clapton, which raised Marley\'s international profile, \"No Woman No Cry\", \"Exodus\", \"Could You Be Loved\", \"Jamming\",\"Redemption Song\" and one of his most famous songs, \"One Love\".[3] His posthumous album Legend (1984) became the best-selling reggae album ever, with sales of more than 12 million copies
