On a cold night in April 1912, over 1,500 people aboard the RMS Titanic lost their lives to the merciless waters of the North Atlantic. More than ninety years after the fateful event critical questions about the ship's destruction still remain.

The mystery of how the ship broke apart and sunk has loomed in the minds of scientists and historians for decades. What actually happened to the ship after striking the iceberg?

A new expedition uncovers missing pieces, and surprising new evidence. Evidence that may forever change what we know about Titanic's final moments.
