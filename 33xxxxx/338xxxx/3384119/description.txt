                                 Release Notes
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Before the Super Bowl became a media-saturated, unofficial "national holiday," it was only a game. The first Super Bowl--pitting the Green Bay Packers against the Kansas City Chiefs--gave scant indication that the game would evolve into a mass-marketing behometh. Using NFL Films footage, newsreels and interviews, this film examines Super Bowl I from several levels: it's awkward efforts to become an "event," the historical impact of the game itself, and the social context of the mid 1960s.

Highlights from game as well as interviews with players of the game from both 
teams.
