Los Relampagos, (spanish for lightning or lightening bolts) was Spain’s No. 1 instrumental rock band during the 1960’s. Melding the sounds of Johnny and the Hurricanes, the Shadows, Duane Eddy, Dick Dale, and other instrumental rock groups with Spanish folk and classical melodies and rhythms, movie themes, and other pop music of the day, Los Relampagos developed a distinctive, highly-musical style that ranks them among the very best instrumental rock groups. Their music is enduring.

From  1962 through 1990, Los Relampagos recorded 13  EP’s, 22 singles, and 9 LP’s on Philips, RCA, Polydor, Novola, and Zafiro, though none were released in the United States.
