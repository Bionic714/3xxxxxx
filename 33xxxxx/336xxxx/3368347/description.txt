            Had to whip 'em a little harder than usual, but
            our finest negro sweat shop employees managed to
            piece together the 98 tracks Century Media split
            this God Forbid album into without too much
            difficulty. Hope you're grateful for all their

            ------------------------------------------------

            On September 20, 2005 Century Media Records will
            release God Forbid's IV: Constitution of
            Treason, their fourth full-length release.
            Produced by Jason Suecof (Trivium) and Eric
            Rachel (Atreyu, Dillinger Escape Plan), the
            album -- a socially and politically charged
            vision of a post-apocalyptic world -- further
            displays the incredible creativity and talent of
            one of the most highly respected bands in metal
            today. To hear the first available music from
            IV: Constitution of Treason, check out the new
            issue of Revolver to find out how you can
            download the album's opening track ("The End of
            the World") as part of the magazine's "Bootleg
            Series".

            Lead guitarist Doc Coyle comments on this highly
            anticipated album: "I'm extremely excited for
            our fans, new and old, to get a listen of this
            album. It's a big step for God Forbid and I feel
            that we have made our most diverse, deep and
            emotional record with out sacrificing heaviness
            or artistic identity. In a current musical
            environment, where there seem to a lot of
            boring, safe records, I feel confident that
            we've continued to take chances while
            challenging ourselves to be a better band. With
            all of that said, we made the record we wanted
            to make, and we couldn't be happier with the
            results."
