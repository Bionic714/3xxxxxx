Ever since I had first heard DJ Shadow's album, Endtroducing, in 1996, 
he had captured my interest with the entrancing melodies and dark soundscapes with such tracks as Organ Donor and Midnight In A Perfect World. Having then learned about Solesides and Quannum, who carried out the new sound of hip-hop and lead the way to new directions for many others throughout the 90s, I was hooked!  As time grew on, the interest had turned into an obsession. With the release of The Private Press in 2002, DJ Shadow had become a God in many headz eyez.  Many remixes and singles later, DJ Shadow was still releasing funk compilations, performing shows alongside Cut Chemist, touring the world, and making many new friends along the way.  

This mix pays tribute to a man who has devoted his life to digging in record bins, spending every waking second listening to music, and forging the progression of music and how we listen to it, Josh Davis, DJ Shadow, take a bow, you deserve it. Outside the Beats, Freaks & Organs covers a 10-year span of DJ Shadow's career and the many styles he has formulated to create his own original sound, so far from anything else out there.
