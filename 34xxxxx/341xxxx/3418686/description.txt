			       -=- Synopsis -=-
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

On March 20, 1995, members of Aum, a small Buddhist sect, released sarin gas
into rush hour crowds in the Tokyo subway system. The sarin, ten times deadlier
than cyanide, killed 12, crippled or injured thousands and much like 9-11 in the
United States, the shock of this attack has forever changed Japanese life.

Director Tatsuya Mori had the opportunity to live with members after the attack
and make this truly unique film illustrating the outside world from the Aum 
perspective. It is an unflinching, uncompromising and wholly neutral document of
how dangerous any highly ordered, strictly administered closed system can 
quickly become - be it the grip of a cult or the societal standards cult members
seek to change.
